What are the revised guidelines on minimum net owned funds (NOF) requirements for NBFCs under the October 2021 scale-based regulation? Do the limits differ by NBFC category?
232 / 500
Answer Instant answer
Under the RBI's Scale-Based Regulation (SBR) framework for NBFCs (effective October 22, 2021), the minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) requirements are tiered by layer classification. NBFC-Base Layer (BL): minimum NOF of ₹2 crore (for new entrants from 2022 onwards; existing NBFCs granted transition until March 2027). NBFC-Middle Layer (ML) and Upper Layer (UL): minimum NOF of ₹10 crore. Investment and Credit Companies (ICC) and Microfinance Institutions (MFI) within the Base Layer also require ₹2 crore. Infrastructure Finance Companies (IFC) categorised in the Middle Layer must maintain a minimum of ₹300 crore.

What is the cooling period for digital loan cancellation under the digital lending guidelines?
The RBI's digital lending guidelines specify that borrowers are entitled to a cooling-off period to exit digital loans. The exact duration is determined by the board-approved policy of the regulated entity, typically ranging from 24 to 72 hours.
